There’s a common saying among India’s (and the world’s) older generations, (think Generation X for India, the Baby Boomers for the world), usually directed at the generation they’re the parents of, (Millennials and Generation Z) which goes something along the lines of “Even I was like you when I was younger and rebellious/a teenager, you will become more conservative/religious as you mature/grow older”, or it’s Hindustani equivalent “Bade hoge toh sab samajh aajayega, bachpan mein sab aise hi hote hai”. The common wisdom (if it can be called that) is that since the same happened to the older generations.
The Baby Boomers: They are the generation born after 1946 till the 1960s, (at least in the United States and most of the Western world. Born out of a period of sudden, yet uncertain and foreboding relative peace, with World War 2 over, and rebuilding commencing in earnest, and progressing throughout, as the first baby boomers neared teenage, the rebuilding was through in many parts of the world, and the world was entering another period of economic affluence, even as the Cold War heated up, causing many of the aforementioned boomers to practice drills on reaching a bunker or a subway in case of a nuclear attack by the opposing side. Other than this, this generation was practically the first in modern times to be able to pursue higher education (on both sides) without the threat of being conscripted and sent off to war (well, until Vietnam and Afghanistan happened, at least). Continuing on with the Americentric point of view, the Sputnik launch in 1957 put the Soviet Union firmly ahead of the West, and kicked off the space race in earnest. The United States’ government, realizing the poor state of education in the country, as well as the need of thousands of specialized STEM majors in order to combat the already rising Soviet Science machine. The Federal government began throwing money at the STEM fields (weirdly reminiscent of the Manhattan project and the sort in World War 2), embarking on a never seen before or after reform in the education system (something emulated by a lot of the West in the coming years), with the Academia reclaiming the importance it was given during the war, with the number of students in college’s continuing to rise even after the veterans had graduated, as the boomers were sent for higher and higher education, leading to the boomers being the most educated generation up until that point in human history. However, all was not well and the Boomers would find themselves in a similar situation in which Gen Z finds itself today. As summarized by Peter Turchin in his hypothesis for social unrest, “elite overproduction” is when a society produces too many potential elite members which it cannot absorb. By the 1970’s, definitely real wages began their decline, income inequality continued to rise, and public debt ballooned to unsustainable numbers.
This is what can be theorized to have led to the baby boomers to what can only be described as revolutionary, especially with the 60’s counterculture, which bloomed out into the mainstream with the Vietnam war, the civil rights’ movement and the second wave of feminism. Woodstock and others of the like also played an important role in bringing the hipster (hippie for short) movement to the front, which rejected materialism, and found solace in spirituality and eastern mysticism, as well as nature. Being pacifists, and consumers of leisurely hallucinogenic drugs and further exploration of sexuality. LGBTQ++ rights activism also picked up, as the younger generations were a lot more open to new experiences and accepting other’s rights to self determination than their forefathers,
The boomer’s counterculture general revolutionary-ish sentiment led to the infamous Jim Crow laws being overturned, allowing for legal equality in the United States between races, and the landmark Roe vs. Wade judgement, effectively banned states from making abortion illegal, (which directly contributed to the fall in crime rate 1990s onwards) but all in all eventually, most of the boomers became the very uptight respectful subservient to authority sell-outs (like their parents before them) who wanted to reinstate the same laws their younger selves and brethren had worked so hard in the 1960's to abolish.
But again, following from the popular quote good times create weak men, weak men create bad times, bad times create strong men and strong men create good times, which can apply to the boomer’s evolution as a generation, continuing on with Americentric view, while their early times were relatively easy, funded on the back of the rebuilding program, the Marshall plan and other strategies, with subsidized housing, education, healthcare and other things, the boomers also inherited what until that point was the largest wealth transfer in history as their parent’s generations started kicking the bucket one by one, which basically led to the revolutionary boomers, who spent their twenties calling for a revolution and an end to war and neoliberalism and consumerism and an N number of endless problems which stem from capitalism, suddenly realised that selfishness is a great strategy for self-fulfilment, and why end war when you can invest in Lockheed Martin and as they bomb the shit out of some Vietcong, you live off the returns for a couple years? Then you reinvest those profits into British Petroleum and enjoy as they drill, extract and frack their way into an unrecoverable situation of global warming?
Other than that little paragraph above, the boomer’s were also hit hard by crises around the world, spending almost their entire young age under the threat of being nuked by the opposing country. Not to mention Watergate, the 1973 recession, Cuban missile crisis, Iran hostage crisis, their heads of state being assassinated, , and all, were some of the things boomers have faced.
Boomers also tend to be the largest population cohort in developed Western countries and the sort due to declining birth rates that followed their generation, which causes multiple problems in economic planning, which can be addressed after acknowledging the fact that boomers are responsible for the economic boom experienced after the dot-com crisis in the 2000s (preceding the Housing crisis, boy we sure do have a lot of crises, don’t we?) when they started earnestly investing into their retirement funds, as most had reached the age to anticipate retirement soon. That was, the largest population cohort in human history yet causing an unprecedented level of growth in the global economy. And that was then. 20 years on, most boomers are moving in to retirement, which means the largest population cohort in history will be pulling their wealth from volatile things like stocks and commodities and will be investing in dependable assets, which don’t really contribute to growth, per se. The effect of this liquidity drying up is of course going to be further compounded by the 2020 Economic Recession caused by the COVID19 pandemic, so all in all, money is going to dry up soon. Another interesting trend among the boomer demographic is that boomers also prefer to spend a lot of their wealth off during their ridiculously long retirement, instead of saving it up to pass it on to their children (On the overall, sizeable inheritances still exist, and for most conventional families, won’t be of much help.
Stepping back to the initial assertion, boomers, while in the early years having been democrats in the United States and overall extremely liberal and somewhat left leaning, progressed to being social and economic conservatives, for which any number of reasons exist, including the explanation that the boomers actually never changed their views, but they remained static, while society, which was overall more conservative before them, had caused them to appear liberal, and with newer generations being ever more liberal than the last, the standards shifted as such to portray the boomers as conservative. Portrayal or not, boomers as a whole tend to vote for right wing movements, being overwhelmingly socially and economically conservative.
Generation Z on the other hand, is overwhelmingly liberal, at least in their early stages of maturity, which is as far as observation is possible. They are a lot less adventurous than earlier generations in their teenage phase, consume less drugs, drink less alcohol and spell a hell of a lot longer time on their smartphones. While early on Generation Z was predicted to be conservative economically given how well the economy was progressing, unemployment was dropping and overall HDI metrics continuing to improve, almost all of these claims were wiped out as Generation Z passed through one of the worst financial recessions in modern history in 2008, and proceeded to see the repair work done on the economy since that over a decade get all but wiped out in the 2020 economic crisis. This leads to a resurgence of the Left in global politics, even after neoliberalism continues to wreck countries, the new generation can still hope.
Socially too, Generation Z is overwhelmingly liberal, although it somewhat varies country to country, but the overall trend is clear. They can still easily be said to be far more liberal right now than the boomers could ever aspire to be. They support same sex marriages, abortions and immigrants, not to mention display an unprecedented amount of trust in science not seen in many generations, also believing in man-made climate change. Thanks to them, religion is on the decline, along with all the social ill that it brings with itself.
Now the Millennials were the first generation which saw economic prospects that were NOT better than their parents, and actually earned on average, less than their parents, despite being much better educated.
Generation Z clearly is going to be the same way, as they move into adulthood and active society en masse, it will be the single largest generational cohort, taking the drunk, drug addled sociopathic boomers out of the driver’s seat in the vehicle that is our society, they will be inheriting a world in crisis, reeling from the first major global pandemic in decades, resurgent right wing nationalistic and populistic politics, increasingly volatile economic situations, and a stressed working class, not to mention an extremely huge pile of debt. Generational debt, not student loans and home mortgages and the sort, a LOT of generational debt. Remember the boomers? Turns out they will have all retired by the time Generation Z starts paying into taxes, and they want their pensions and 401ks and more, in effect, still wielding a deathgrip on our socioeconomic state and society, refusing to let go after overdosing in the driver’s seat.
But, of course, keep in mind the fact that Gen Z are going to be the largest population cohort in human history, and they WILL have political control, which if they vote as united as the boomers did, they can wield to emerge through all the crises they inherit from the boomers, or they can just die off due to irreversible climate change that will become very real in the next 50 years. Thanks Grandpa!
